_**Jodorowsky's Dune**_ is a 2013 American-French [documentary film](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Documentary_film) directed by [Frank Pavich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frank_Pavich). The film explores cult film director [Alejandro Jodorowsky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alejandro_Jodorowsky)'s [unsuccessful attempt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dune_(Jodorowsky_film)) to adapt and film [Frank Herbert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frank_Herbert)'s 1965 [science fiction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science_fiction) novel [_Dune_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dune_(novel)) in the mid-1970s.

## Background[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Jodorowsky%27s_Dune&action=edit&section=1)]

In 1971, the production company Apjac International (APJ) headed by film producer [Arthur P. Jacobs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_P._Jacobs) optioned the [rights to film](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Film_rights) _Dune_. However, Jacobs died in 1973 before a film could be developed.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune)

In December 1974, a French consortium led by Jean-Paul Gibon purchased the film rights from APJ, with director [Alejandro Jodorowsky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alejandro_Jodorowsky) set to direct.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune) Along with French producer [Michel Seydoux](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michel_Seydoux), Jodorowsky proceeded to approach, among others, [Virgin Records](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virgin_Records) with the [prog rock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Progressive_rock) groups [Tangerine Dream](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tangerine_Dream), [Gong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gong_(band)) and [Mike Oldfield](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mike_Oldfield) before settling on [Pink Floyd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pink_Floyd) and [Magma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magma_(band)) for some of the music; artists [H. R. Giger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H._R._Giger), [Chris Foss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chris_Foss) and [Jean Giraud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_Giraud) for set and character design; [Dan O'Bannon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dan_O%27Bannon) for special effects; and [Salvador Dalí](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salvador_Dal%C3%AD), [Orson Welles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orson_Welles), [Gloria Swanson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gloria_Swanson), [David Carradine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Carradine), [Mick Jagger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mick_Jagger), [Udo Kier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Udo_Kier), [Amanda Lear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amanda_Lear) and others for the cast.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune) Jodorowsky intended his son [Brontis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brontis_Jodorowsky), 12 years old at the start of pre-production, to star as [Paul Atreides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Atreides).

Herbert traveled to Europe in 1976 to find that $2 million of the $9.5 million budget had already been spent in pre-production and that Jodorowsky's script would result in a 14-hour film ("It was the size of a phone book", Herbert later recalled). Jodorowsky took creative liberties with the source material, but Herbert said that he and Jodorowsky had an amicable relationship.[[_citation needed_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed)] After two and a half years in development, the project ultimately stalled for financial reasons since $5 million was still missing to round off the $15-million total budget.

After the film rights lapsed in 1982, they were purchased by Italian producer [Dino De Laurentiis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dino_De_Laurentiis), who eventually released the 1984 film [_Dune_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dune_(1984_film)), directed by [David Lynch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Lynch).

## Content[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Jodorowsky%27s_Dune&action=edit&section=2)]

Acclaimed comic artist Jean "Moebius" Giraud worked with Jodorowsky to create a storyboard composed of 3000 drawings that depicted the entire film.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune)

Salvador Dalí was set to play the Emperor and claimed he wanted to be the highest-paid actor in Hollywood history. He asked for $100,000 per hour to act in the movie. Jodorowsky proposed to pay him $100,000 per minute, but then reduced the Emperor's scenes so that Dalí would be needed for no more than 3 to 5 minutes with the rest of his lines spoken by a robotic lookalike; Dalí consented in exchange for being allowed to keep the "doll" Emperor for his museum.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune)

Jodorowsky's refusal to compromise on _Dune'_s running time was one main reason the film did not get made. Hollywood did not want the film's length to exceed two hours. Jodorowsky felt 10 to 14 hours would be more appropriate for the adaptation.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune)

The film notes that Jodorowsky's script, extensive storyboards, and concept art were sent to all major film studios, and argues that these influenced and inspired later film productions, including [_Star Wars_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_Wars), the [_Alien_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alien_(franchise)) series, [_Flash Gordon_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flash_Gordon_(film)), the [_Terminator_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terminator_(franchise)) series, and [_The Fifth Element_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Fifth_Element).[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune) In particular, the Jodorowsky-assembled team of O'Bannon, Foss, Giger, and Giraud went on to collaborate on the 1979 film [_Alien_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alien_(film)).[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune)

"It was a great undertaking to do the script," Jodorowsky says in the film. Speaking of Herbert's novel, he says: "It's very, it's like Proust, I compare it to great literature."

The documentary concludes that Jodorowsky's efforts did not go to waste, and that he and Giraud recycled much of their concepts for [_The Incal_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Incal), a series of graphic novels that began publishing in 1980.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune)

## Production[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Jodorowsky%27s_Dune&action=edit&section=3)]

The project was officially announced in May 2011.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune) Director Pavich filmed an extensive series of interviews with the principal players involved in the failed 1970s adaptation, shooting in France, Switzerland, the United Kingdom, and the United States.

## Release[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Jodorowsky%27s_Dune&action=edit&section=4)]

_Jodorowsky's Dune_ [premiered](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Film_premiere) at the Director's Fortnight at the [2013 Cannes Film Festival](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2013_Cannes_Film_Festival) in May 2013.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune) [Sony Pictures Classics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sony_Pictures_Classics) acquired the North American distribution rights to the film in July 2013,[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune) and later announced a theatrical release date of March 7, 2014.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune) The film was released on DVD and on-demand on July 8, 2014.

## Reception[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Jodorowsky%27s_Dune&action=edit&section=5)]

The film has received critical acclaim. [_Variety_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Variety_(magazine)) called it a "mind-blowing cult movie" and said that director Pavich "happens upon a compelling theory: that even in its still-born form, the film manifested the sort of collective [consciousness] that Jodorowsky was trying to peddle through its plot, trickling down to influence other sci-fi films that followed".[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune) [_The Hollywood Reporter_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter) declared the "entertaining documentary makes the case for this overblown epic as a legendary lost masterpiece".[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune) [_Entertainment Weekly_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly) named _Jodorowsky's Dune_ as one of its 10 Best Movies of 2014.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune)

Review aggregation website [Rotten Tomatoes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes) gave _Jodorowsky's Dune_ a 98% approval rating based on reviews from 121 critics, with an [average rating](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weighted_arithmetic_mean) of 8/10. The site's consensus states: "Part thoughtful tribute, part bittersweet reminder of a missed opportunity, _Jodorowsky's Dune_ offers a fascinating look at a lost sci-fi legend."[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune) [Metacritic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metacritic) gives the film a 79/100 rating based on 31 critics, indicating "generally favorable reviews".[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jodorowsky%27s_Dune)
