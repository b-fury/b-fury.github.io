# **Beyond Meat - Firm Analysis**

## **Introduction**

Beyond Meat  is a Los Angeles-based company that specializes in plant-based meat substitutes. The company was founded in 2009, launched its initial product (originally named "Chicken-Free Strips") at Whole Foods in 2012, and expanded nationally in 2013. Beyond Meat went public in 2019, becoming the first plant-based meat company to do so.

Today they are a leader in the plant-based industry with a range of plant-based products designed to mimic the taste, texture, and appearance of meats like poultry, beef, and pork. Their [mission](https://www.beyondmeat.com/en-US/mission) is to help people shift from animal products to plant-based meat to “positively affect the planet, the environment, the climate, and even ourselves.”

## **Industry Analysis**

The plant-based meat industry has seen significant growth in recent years. The industry has seen an increase in popularity for various sustainability reasons, health concerns, religious/ethical reasons, and for environmental and  animal rights. According to [EY](https://www.ey.com/en_us/strategy/how-alternative-proteins-are-reshaping-meat-industries), the meat alternative market was worth more than $14 billion USD and it’s projected to reach more than $17 billion by  2027.

Beyond Meat faces competition from several companies. The competitive landscape has become more crowded as consumer demand for meat alternatives rises. Competition in the space has led to increased innovation and improved options for consumers.

# **Beyond Meat’s Competitive Strategy**

Beyond Meat employs a differentiation strategy, aiming to stand out with a unique offering in the fast-growing plant-based protein market. The company has [invested](https://www.trefis.com/no-login-required/eR7WVH6T/Beyond-Meat-Expenses-How-Does-Beyond-Meat-Spend-Its-Money-) heavily in R&D to create plant-based meats that not only imitate the taste and texture of animal-derived meats but also meet increasing consumer demand for more sustainable and humane alternatives. This has led to partnerships with high-profile fast-food chains and supermarkets, effectively broadening its consumer base from vegans and vegetarians to include "flexitarians" who still eat meat but are looking to reduce their consumption.

In essence, Beyond Meat's competitive strategy hinges on creating a high-quality, recognizable brand that can command premium pricing. Furthermore, they are making strides towards scalability by constantly optimizing their production processes, thereby aiming to bring costs down over time.

Marketing is another cornerstone of their strategy. The brand has been skillful in leveraging social media and celebrity endorsements to reach a younger, more health-conscious demographic. Beyond Meat also emphasizes its sustainability credentials, aligning itself with broader societal shifts towards environmental responsibility and animal welfare. In addition, the company is pursuing international expansion, recognizing the global demand for alternative protein sources.

The competitive landscape has not deterred Beyond Meat from innovating; rather, it continues to iterate on its product line by adding new flavors, formats, and even different types of plant-based protein sources. By staying ahead in the innovation curve and fortifying its brand as a leader in plant-based meats, Beyond Meat aims to fend off competition from both start-ups and traditional food companies venturing into the plant-based arena. As Beyond Meat, Inc. continues to make inroads into the burgeoning plant-based protein market, it faces both opportunities and challenges.

## **Competitive advantages Beyond Meat has:**

- **The superior product:** Beyond Meat's products includes plant-based chicken, burgers, meatballs, and sausages. Each product is made from various plant-based ingredients, including rice, pea, and mung bean protein - carefully selected to replicate the taste and texture of real meat.
- **Health benefits:** Beyond Meat products are often viewed as a healthier alternative to real meat because they are lower in cholesterol/saturated fat and free from hormones/antibiotics.
- **Market expansion and R&D:** Beyond Meat has rapidly expanded its presence in the food industry by making their products available at many grocery stores, restaurants, and fast-food chains in the US and around the world. They also have invested heavily in research and development to continually improve and expand their product line.
- **Sustainability:** Beyond Meat’s mission is to reduce the environmental impact of meat production. Their products require less resources (like land, water, and energy) and generate fewer greenhouse gas compared to traditional meat. STAT needed about how much goes into a cow.
- **IPO:**  Beyond Meat went public in May 2019, making it the first plant-based meat company to be publicly traded. Their Wall Street debut was very successful - reflecting a growing consumer interest in plant-based alternatives.

## **Market Environment Analysis**

According to [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/company/press/plant-based-foods-market-to-hit-162-billion-in-next-decade-projects-bloomberg-intelligence/), the global market for plant-based protein is expected to reach $35 billion by 2026. This growth is driven by consumer awareness of the environmental impact of animal farming, health concerns related to meat consumption, and cultural shifts towards plant-based diets. Beyond Meat is poised to capture a significant share of this expanding market.

## **SWOT Analysis**

Beyond Meat has shown considerable **strength** in their ability to disrupt the traditional meat industry by offering plant-based alternatives that closely mimic the taste and texture of animal meat. This innovation has allowed it to penetrate not only the retail market but also food service channels, [partnering](https://www.vox.com/future-perfect/2019/10/10/20870872/where-to-buy-impossible-foods-beyond-meat) with high-profile brands like McDonald's, Subway, and KFC. The company's brand is also aligned with growing consumer trends that prioritize sustainability, health, and ethical considerations in food choices.

However, there are **weaknesses** that Beyond Meat must address. One of the significant challenges is the premium pricing of its products, making them less accessible for the average consumer. This limits market reach and could slow down mass adoption. Moreover, as the product mainly consists of processed ingredients, it has received criticism for not being as healthy as whole, plant-based foods.

**Opportunities** for Beyond Meat are abundant. The increasing awareness of climate change, animal welfare, and the need for sustainable food systems provides a fertile market for expansion. Emerging markets, especially in Asia and Europe, are becoming more open to plant-based diets, presenting potential for international growth. Moreover, the company can diversify its product line to include different types of plant-based proteins and foods, thus widening its appeal.

**Threats** facing Beyond Meat include increasing competition, both from startups and established food companies that are entering the plant-based protein market. Regulatory hurdles could also pose a challenge, as the labeling and marketing of plant-based products are subject to change and scrutiny. Finally, there is the risk of consumer perception turning against processed plant-based foods in favor of whole foods, which could impact the company's growth trajectory.

### **Financial Analysis**

Beyond Meat, Inc., one of the frontrunners in the plant-based meat alternatives sector, has seen a transformative trajectory from 2018 to 2022. Following its IPO in May 2019, the company witnessed an impressive surge in its revenue, growing from about $88 million in 2018 to approximately $325 million by 2020. This robust growth in revenue, however, didn't always translate to consistent profitability (see chart below) , as significant investments were channeled into research & development, marketing, and expansion campaigns, leading to fluctuations in net income. The company's gross margins also experienced volatility during this period. With the increased scaling of production, there was an initial boost in gross margins, but they later faced pressures from supply chain disruptions and an influx of competition.

Speaking of competition, Beyond Meat's early entry into the plant-based meat space granted it remarkable brand recognition. But the landscape soon grew crowded with a mix of budding startups like Impossible Foods and established food industry titans such as Kellogg's MorningStar Farms and Tyson Foods, all vying for a piece of the plant-based pie. Beyond Meat's public trading status provided it a unique advantage in accessing capital compared to newer startups. However, it faced the might of established conglomerates with their expansive distribution networks, even though these giants sometimes lacked the nimbleness and brand specificity inherent to companies like Beyond Meat.

## **Other companies in the plant-based meat industry:**

- **Impossible Foods,** which launched in 2016 and is known for their plant-based burger. They have also made ground meat and sausage alternatives and partnered with numerous restaurants/fast-food chains.
- **MorningStar Farms,** a subsidiary of Kellogg, rolled out its first vegetarian product — the MorningStar Farms Veggie Burger — in 1975. They offer an array of veggie burgers, fake chicken nuggets, and breakfast items for vegetarians and “flexitarians.”
- **Tofurky,** founded in 1980, best known for its Tofurky Roast — a vegetarian and vegan alternative to traditional Thanksgiving turkey. Over the years, the company has expanded its product line to include alternatives for sausages, hot dogs, burgers and even deli slices and meatless ground meat.

**Larger environment analysis:**

It’s important to note that Beyond Meat doesn’t just compete in the plant-based meat industry but also with the meat industry at large. In fact, Beyond Meat and the rise of alternative proteins impacted the meat industry — a global sector that was [valued at](https://www.statista.com/statistics/502286/global-meat-and-seafood-market-value/#:~:text=The%20value%20of%20the%20global,billion%20U.S.%20dollars%20by%202027.&text=The%20production%20volume%20of%20meat,around%20328%20million%20metric%20tons.) $897 billion USD  in 2021.

Additionally, the alternative meat industry won’t necessarily replace the traditional meat industry; it merely offers consumers more choices — choices Beyond Meat would argue are more ethical, sustainable, and healthy. However, market perception and consumer education [still remain](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0950329320303323) big barriers to alternative meat adoption.

Other competitors in adjacent spaces include plant-based eggs (like [JUST egg](https://www.ju.st/)), [plant-based seafood](https://vegnews.com/taste-tests/vegan-seafood-products), and the plant-based protein powder/beverage industries.

## **Challenges the plant-based meat industry faces:**

While the plant-based meat industry has experienced growth it also faces various challenges, including:

- **Taste and Texture:** One of the primary challenges for plant-based meat companies is replicating the taste and texture of traditional meat. The Washington Post [interviewed experts](https://www.washingtonpost.com/climate-solutions/2023/01/19/plant-based-meat-failing/) that hypothesized that copying the taste/texture of real meat will make it harder for the industry to grow.
- **Consumer Perception:** Many potential consumers have preconceived notions about the taste, quality, and nutritional value of plant-based meat - a large barrier to adoption. Companies need to work on effective packaging, marketing, and education to address these misconceptions.
- **Cost:** Plant-based meat products can be more expensive to produce than traditional meat due to the cost of ingredients (such as pea protein) and specialized processing methods. Reducing production costs while maintaining quality is an industry challenge.
- **Regulatory/Labeling Challenges:** The industry faces ongoing regulatory challenges related to product labeling and naming conventions. Many traditional meat associations, such as National Cattlemen's Beef Association (NCBA) and National Pork Producers Council (NPPC), [argue for stricter labeling](https://www.ncba.org/ncba-news/news-releases/news/details/30637/cattle-industry-fights-controversial-sec-climate-rule) rules to differentiate plant-based products from animal-based ones, which can impact consumer recognition and acceptance.
- **Nutritional Profile:** While many plant-based products are designed to be healthier than their animal-based counterparts, some are high in sodium, saturated fat, and other less-healthy ingredients.

Despite these challenges, the Good Food Institute [found that](https://gfi.org/marketresearch/) the plant-based meat industry in the US saw sales increase 7% in 2022, mirroring the animal-based food sector. While sales increased (much thanks to inflation), the GFI found that most plant-based companies claimed the number of units sold declined.

Additionally, their study found that in 2022, 60% of US households purchased plant-based foods, a similar percent to the prior year. This number suggests that Beyond Meat and other plant-based companies need to get more people trying their product.

**Strategic Recommendations**

One of the primary challenges Beyond Meat faces is the high cost of its products, which acts as a barrier to entry for many consumers. To make its products more accessible, the company should invest in supply chain efficiencies and economies of scale to reduce costs. In addition to this, diversifying its product line could open up new avenues for growth. While Beyond Meat has had success with burgers, sausages, and ground meat, introducing plant-based alternatives to other popular animal products like fish, chicken, or dairy could lessen the company's dependency on a few core items.

Global expansion, particularly into emerging markets where meat consumption is on the rise, should also be a focus. These markets are becoming increasingly aware of the environmental impact of meat consumption, making them ripe for plant-based alternatives. Additionally, Beyond Meat could further differentiate itself by optimizing the nutritional content of its products. Though already considered a healthier alternative, reducing sodium, saturated fats, or artificial ingredients could strengthen its market position.

In the retail sector, the company has had some success in food service, but there's room for growth in retail partnerships. Special promotions, bundled products, or even co-branding with grocery chains can enhance brand visibility and encourage consumer adoption.

Sustainability is another key area where Beyond Meat can excel. Continuous investment in sustainable practices across product development, packaging, transportation, and overall operations could enhance brand loyalty among eco-conscious consumers. Transparency in sustainability efforts through certifications and reports could lend credibility to these initiatives.

Consumer education should also be a priority. The transition to a plant-based diet involves a learning curve, and educational marketing could help consumers better understand how to cook and enjoy plant-based foods, thereby reducing barriers to adoption. As competition intensifies from both startups and established food giants, Beyond Meat should consider defensive strategies such as faster innovation cycles, aggressive intellectual property strategies, and exclusive partnerships with retailers and food service providers.

To tailor its marketing and product development efforts, leveraging data analytics could be invaluable. This would allow the company to better understand consumer preferences, buying patterns, and regional trends. Lastly, given the regulatory scrutiny surrounding the labeling and marketing of plant-based foods, Beyond Meat should be proactive in policy and advocacy. Engaging in these discussions could not only safeguard the company's interests but also shape industry standards in a way that is favorable to them.

## **Key Risks**

The plant-based meat market is becoming more competitive, with new startups entering the fray alongside traditional food giants like Nestlé and Tyson Foods. These competitors could have advantages in areas such as distribution, cost structure, and brand recognition. Beyond Meat also faces regulatory challenges, particularly around the labeling and advertising of plant-based foods. Laws that prohibit the use of the term "meat" for plant-based products could create obstacles in brand recognition and consumer understanding.

Consumer perception plays a significant role in Beyond Meat's success, as its products are often viewed as healthier and more sustainable than traditional animal meats. However, any study or controversy that challenges these perceptions [could affect](https://www.nytimes.com/2021/10/15/business/beyond-meat-impossible-emissions.html) sales negatively. As Beyond Meat continues to scale, it faces supply chain risks such as disruptions due to political turmoil, climatic conditions, or pandemics, which could have implications for both production and profitability. Price sensitivity is another concern; the relatively high cost of Beyond Meat products could limit market penetration, especially in price-sensitive markets or during economic downturns.

Health concerns also loom large. Despite being plant-based, Beyond Meat's products are processed foods containing ingredients like soy or pea protein isolates and various additives. With a growing consumer trend toward whole, natural foods, this could pose a risk. Intellectual property is another critical factor, as the company's proprietary technology gives its products a unique texture and flavor. Any IP theft or challenges could seriously compromise its competitive edge.

Expanding into international markets presents its own set of challenges, including currency fluctuations, geopolitical issues, and variances in consumer preferences and regulatory environments. Quality control is essential as the company broadens its product range and increases production. Any major product recalls or quality issues could severely damage the brand. Beyond Meat also relies heavily on partnerships with fast-food chains and retailers; losing a significant partner could substantially impact revenue and brand visibility. Lastly, cultural barriers may slow down adoption in markets where meat consumption is deeply ingrained in the culture, potentially affecting the company's international growth prospects.

## **Key Actions**

To effectively navigate the challenges it faces, Beyond Meat must employ a varied strategy. To counteract the intensifying competition, the company should consider diversifying its product range, not only by offering different meat substitutes but by exploring other food industry sectors as well. At the same time, an active engagement in policy discussions and lobbying for more favorable regulations can allow the company to shape the conversation around plant-based products. Emphasizing transparency in their marketing campaigns will not only bolster their consumer perception but also underline the health and environmental advantages of their offerings.

Enhancing their supply chain, through strategic partnerships, varied sourcing, and vertical integration, is essential to mitigate potential disruptions. Additionally, in response to the prevalent price sensitivity in the market, Beyond Meat can aim to refine production processes, achieve larger economies of scale, and possibly introduce products at varied price ranges, catering to different consumer groups. Their commitment to quality and innovation can be further cemented by continuous R&D efforts, ensuring they are at the cutting edge of the plant-based movement. Intellectual property, being central to their offering, should be consistently reviewed and strengthened to safeguard their unique products.

As Beyond Meat eyes international territories, a localized strategy, from tailored marketing to product adaptations, will be key to resonate with diverse markets. Quality assurance, fostered through rigorous control systems, staff training, and a fortified supplier relationship, will ensure product consistency. Deepening ties with their partners, understanding local dietary inclinations, and culturally adapting their products can help them penetrate markets where traditional meat consumption is entrenched. An agile response mechanism, fed by constant consumer feedback, alongside a structured risk management framework, can help Beyond Meat preemptively address challenges. Finally, by regularly engaging in scenario planning, the company can foresee and prepare for various industry disruptions.

By embracing these strategies, Beyond Meat is poised to fortify its market stance, anchor its sustainability, and set the stage for enduring growth.
