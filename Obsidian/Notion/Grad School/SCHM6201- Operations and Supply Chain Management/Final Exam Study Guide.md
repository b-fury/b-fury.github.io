**SCHM 6201 - EXAM REVIEW GUIDE**

  

**NOTE**:  This guide only provides an overview of the main topics and key concepts you need to understand. Exam questions are set in a way that they test your ability to demonstrate your understanding of the concepts and their applications in business practice.

**Understanding Supply Chain and Operations Management**

- Importance of SCM in creating value and as a source of competitive advantage
- Components of operations systems
- Understanding of the following concepts as they relate to operations and supply chain management
    - Bullwhip effect, Just-in-Time, Bottleneck, Process capacity

**Operations Processes**

- Understanding of the basic types of processes (e.g., job shop, batch, flexible manufacturing, etc.)
- How to improve operations processes
- Quality and its dimensions
- Quality management approaches and awards

**Supply Chain Processes**

- Understanding of inventory management
    - Role of inventory and impact of inventory on financial performance
    - Inventory control policies – including when to and how to order; measures of performance, etc.
    - Approaches for managing inventory (e.g., ABC analysis, VMI, etc.)
- Demand forecasting
    - Forecasting procedures (qualitative and quantitative methods – e.g., moving average, etc.)
    - How to evaluate forecast performance (accuracy and bias)
- Understanding of aggregate planning
    - Objective of aggregate planning including costs to consider
    - Basic aggregate plans – e.g., level, chase, mixed
    - Sales and operations planning (S&OP)

**Managing Supplies**

- Understand the importance of supply management
    - Understanding of key considerations in supply management
    - Considerations in make vs. buy decisions
    - Managing supplier relationships (why relationships matter)
    - How to assess and select suppliers
    - Approaches for improving supply management
- Understanding of risk management
    - Challenges in managing supply chain risks (including IP)
    - How to mitigate supply chain risks

**Logistics Management and Supply Chain Strategy**

- Logistics management and its contribution to value creation
- Understanding of transportation management
    - Role of transportation management
    - Transportation economics, pricing, regulation, and consolidation
    - Different modes of transportation
    - Considerations in mode selection and rates
    - Determinants of transportation rates and modal choice
- Understanding of role of distribution management in supply chain
    - Economic, service benefits
    - Factors influencing logistics/distribution network design and location decisions
    - Logistics outsourcing – e.g., service providers/3PLs and their strategies
