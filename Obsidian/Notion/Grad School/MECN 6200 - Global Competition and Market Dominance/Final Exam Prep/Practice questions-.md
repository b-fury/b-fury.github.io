    1. Marginal revenue - money to be made on the next unit sold
    2. Marginal cost - cost to make next unit
2. When is the following equation used? MR = P *(1-1/n) (Remember n = elasticity)
    1. To calculate the marginal revenue to be made given a markets structures
3. How can a firm maximize its profits?
    1. by selling at a price greater than average total cost if the market allows, if not, then by setting marginal cost to marginal revenue
4. Explain why a change in a firm’s total fixed cost of production will shift its average total cost curve, but not its marginal cost curve. average total cost is fixed cost + variable costs, so they are separate

    1. Sherman act:
        1. cant have or conspire to have a monopoly
        2. cant restrict trade
    2. Clayton Act
        1. cant descriminate on pricing due to quantity or other barriers (false scarcity)
    3. FTC Act
        1. cant have unfair competition
6. Define Increasing/Decreasing returns to scale.
    1. Increasing change in output is greater than change in input
    2. decreasing: change in output is less than change in input
7. What are the different ways of measuring market structure?
    1. monopoly, mc, oligopoly, competitive
8. What are the characteristics of the 4 market structures (# of sellers, barriers to entry, pricing strategy, product differentiation, and examples of each)?
    1. monopoly: 1 seller, high barriers, highest price possible, no differentiation, MacBook
    2. Oligopoly: few sellers, high barriers, ==cartel pricing, no or limited differentiation: airlines==
    3. Monolpolistic competition: a lot of sellers, low barriers, pricing and quality, restaurants
    4. competitive: infinte sellers, no barriers, pricing is the differentiation, lowest prices, wheat growers

11. What is Price Discrimination? How are the degrees of Price Discrimination used differently?
    1. the ability to charge different pricing for different scenarios
        1. 1st degree: charging consumer most theyre willing to pay: coupons, loyalty memberships
        2. 2nd degree: charging with a sense of urgency, i.e. raising prices based on demand: uber, off-peak power
        3. 3rd degree: charging indifferent regions based on demand in each place: Plane tickets
