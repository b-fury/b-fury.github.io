### Money and the Federal Reserve

**Monetary Base** (M0) = Cash + Coins (currency) + Reserves

**M1** = Monetary Base + Demand Deposits, Traveler’s Checks, etc.

**M2** = M1 + Savings Deposits, Money Market Funds, etc.

**M3** = M2 + Large CDs

**L** = M3 + Commercial Paper + U.S. Savings Bonds

### Money Creation

Loans create money

**Money Multiplier** is the multiple by which deposits increase for every dollar increase in reserves.

**Money supply = $20,000**

**What happens to the deposit?**

- $4,000 stays at the bank as _reserves_
- $16,000 is available for _loans_.

Someone borrows $16,000 to buy a car.

The money supply is now equal to $36,000: $20,000 plus $16,000; what you deposited plus the loan. The loan _**created**_ money.

The car dealer deposits the $16,000 in a bank, bringing the money supply to $48,000: $20,000 plus $16,000 plus $12,800; what you deposited plus what the car dealer deposited, and now $12,800 deposited by whoever received the new loan funds. The loan _**created**_ money.
