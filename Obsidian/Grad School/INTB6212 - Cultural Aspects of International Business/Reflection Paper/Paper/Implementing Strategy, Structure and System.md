While it is important to understand the cultural context of your employees, you must also know how to implement change within your organization.

It was very interesting to me to read about Lou Gerstner and his success at IBM. As a quick reminder, Gerstner helped to bring IBM back from the brink of irrelevance in the 90's by implementing a renewed focus and business plan that focused on customer groups instead of the more conventional method of simply building the best products and having the custom come to you. Gerstner was formerly from McKinsey, where they have long advocated for the 7S change model (**See footnotes )**. It is highly likely that this influenced Gerstner's path. Gerstner touched all 7 fundamental tenets of the 7S's. He sharpened and refocused the hard skills refining the business strategy to be more company focused, unifying internal structures so there could be better cohesion throughout the globe, and improved (or otherwise purchased) better systems. Unfortunately he met a lot of resistance in improving the soft skills, as each culture and territory wanted to compete geographically for success.

## My Experience at Thermo Fisher Scientific (and How this course Helps)

It has been very interesting for me to go through this course and learn about all the different ways a global company can operate. It has made me really appreciate and reflect upon how well Thermo handles globalization. On a near daily basis I am working with employees from all around the world, today alone I have had meetings with people in China, Germany, India, and the UK, and it felt completely normal. Here at Thermo we have an innovation culture, and we are completely motivated by internal improvements and delivering the best products to our customers.

One of our core values is Integrity. Here we are all constantly reminded that the work we do matters, and just as importantly. how we do the work matters. We have a culture of openness, and simply targeting good products leaving the door. Unlike in pre-Nadella Microsoft, there are not perverse incentives for doing better than coworkers.

Another core value that is very important to this course is Involvement. It is one of our corporate values to make sure that each person is treated with respect, and that we all work as a Global team. This allows us to take into consideration the varying viewpoints from all the different cultures and values our employee base possesses. Most, if not all, of our leadership (and us employees) has had face-to-face with all of the global teams in the business unit that we will be working with. This allows for a deeper understanding of our culture, and reinforces what we saw in the Cushy Armchair case: that there is no greater way to learn a culture, than to immerse yourself in it.
