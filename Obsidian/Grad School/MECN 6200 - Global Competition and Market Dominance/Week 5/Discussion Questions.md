["Taylor Swift's Ticket Strategy: Brilliant Business or Slowing Demand?" (Links to an external site.)](https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/taylor-swifts-ticket-strategy-brilliant-business-or-slowing-demand-630218/) _Rolling Stone_

### Questions

1. As a consumer, are you disappointed that the artist is seeking to recapture the gains going to ticket scalpers?
2. If you plan to attend a concert what strategies will you consider as to when to purchase your tickets?

I am not concerned by this, assuming a closer-to-concert-date ticket price doesn't go up. I think scalpers are very much a parasitic business, and typically scalping agencies and aftermarket sellers have purchased all of the tickets before consumers could. The demand obviously set the price higher, and these scalpers made a business off of that market correction. I fully support artists trying to get money, but I think the elephant in the room here is Ticketmaster/Livenation. These companies not only have essentially a monopoly on the ticket market, but they also own major venues in major markets (like the House of Blues), which forces artists to not only use their ticketing platform, but also their venues. There is no guarantee that the artists receive the ticket fees, and depending on their contract, artists will get a per-show flat rate [2]. Kid Rock also tried a new method capping ticket and drink prices [3]. This deal only worked because of partnerships with both Ticketmaster and Live Nation. Therefore the venues were already owned by these companies, and Ticketmaster still charged a $5 fee on top of the ticket price, with Kid Rock taking a revenue share cut and made partnership deals on all merch, product sales, beverages and food items.

If there was more competition in the ticket buying business in a primary market, then there is a chance that competition would lower prices. If we take supporting artists out of this equation then this shift is giving Ticketmaster more monopolistic power. This will only further increase prices, and keep competition to a minimum.

Personally, I tend to go to smaller shows at venues that have non-Ticketmaster run ticketing pages. I think that if I were more affected by this I would absolutely be less likely to go to shows. It is also entirely possible that my decision to go to smaller shows as a college student was in itself motivated by trying to lower costs and still see live music. I tend to try to book concerts I want to see as soon as tickets are available. Know that pricing strategies are changing, than I may decide to wait longer to buy a ticket.

### Discussion 2

### Questions

1. Companies like Google, Apple and Facebook are facing intense antitrust scrutiny. Do you feel that the lack of competition in their markets is harmful to you, those you know, or society?
2. Should these companies be left alone?

I think that each companies position is quite different. I think that Google certainly has a monopoly on search (although some competitors are popping up like DuckDuckGo), but they also have a near monopoly on browsers with almost 70% share [2]. This means that people are funneled to more of Google's services, and advertisements, which does affect my day-to-day. I think that overall Google's monopoly does not hurt me, personally, however it dominates the ad market, which affects all levels of internet commerce. Chances are very high that you have seen several ads sold by Google today alone. I personally do not believe that google has to be affected _yet_. If they continue to further hide search results in favor of more ad space [6], then I think the DOJ should move in, as that is using ones monopoly power to further make revenue.

Apple is in an interesting position when it comes to antitrust. There are several facets to this issue, and I think they they all hold some merit, but not necessarily the way they are presented. For instance, Epic has filed complaints against apple having a monopoly on their app store [7], which I think is a totally misguided approach. Apple's app store is unique to its phones, but it is not unique to the global cell phone market. For instance, Android phones (which are globally more popular) have the Google Play Store. In some cases there are also other default app stores, but it depends on whether the vendor goes off the Android Open Source Project, or runs the main Android builds. I do not believe that the app store itself is anticompetitive. Users are free to choose a smartphone from many vendors other than Apple if they don't want the Apple App Store. I think the best case to be made against Apple is that they have placed sales pitches for their other services in the device itself. I have seen on my own phone Apple trying to sell me Apple arcade, and the Apple Music app experience is horrendous unless you subscribe to their streaming service. This is no different than Microsoft shipping internet explorer as the default browser in the early Windows days which saw them getting put on trial for antitrust reasons. It is for these reasons I think that Apple needs to be investigated for antitrust complaints.

In the case of Facebook, I am personally no longer a facebook user, but I have seen first-hand how Facebook's algorithm can directly affect its users, and that data has been researched extensively over the years [3][4]. This, however is not the monopoly in question (information flow does not seem to be related to these antitrust complaints). The biggest issue at hand is the collection and usage of data to sell ads. Much like Google's Antitrust issues, Facebook has carved itself a niche where it knows information about individuals and tries to sell them things. The basis of the most recent complaint is that Facebook has aggregated too many social platforms, and now wants to share data between them to link even more personal information together [5]. This creates a monopoly as some of the largest platforms which were once disparate are now under one ad space. I think that regulators should step in and affect this, as Facebook hold so much personal data on a user that hyper-targeted ads may become damaging to ones psyche.
