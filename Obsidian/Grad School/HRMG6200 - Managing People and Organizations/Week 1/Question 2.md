Bob Koski's main assumptions [1] to get the best work out of employees were the following: horizontal management, eliminating intimidation, clear operation communications, group self management, and a collaborative decision making process. What this meant in practice is that the majority of sources of conflict between the company and its employees was eliminated. In horizontal management, Bob found a may to allow for all employees to be on the same functional plane. There was no institutional structure to create a high/low power dynamic. This allowed for staff at all skill and career levels to collaborate, providing useful feedback to younger employees, but also enabling them to learn and provide new insights.

The biggest assumption was giving employees the power to write their own job descriptions, or put more simply, the ability to define you own job. This, in my opinion, leads directly to providing autonomy and the desire to seek mastery that Pink [2] says is so crucial to motivating employees. They key for this to be most successful is for the workers to be highly skilled, and technically sophisticated. As a company that designs and manufacturers hydraulic parts, it is safe to assume its workers fit this description. The employees were given a much larger sense of purpose. By collectively taking place in this new business experiment employees were far more motivated and empowered to try something new. This is the group self-management aspect. Functional groups would pop up, sa a manufacturing cohort was established, and rthey collectively would define how products would be assembled. This allowed for rapid, iterative production improvements, instead of having to re-steer an entire production organization.

This lead to a company that either met or exceeded its goals in the long run, and is even still a successful business today.

My company incorporates some of these methods. First, I have been made to feel like I have autonomy. A significant portion of my time weekly is self-direct research and design projects. The goal here is that I will develop a technology that will create a new market, not just expand an existing one. I am also in a rather flat organization. I report directly to the head of R&D. This means that I need not worry about having a manager in my specialization bothering me, which allows me to have very few barriers to creation. Last, I have a large amount of freedom in my schedule. I set my work hours, and even set my work location. This is partially due to covid, but my entire office is open and people are on-site most days a week. I am fortunate enough to be able to dictate when that will be. All of these factors combine to give me a feeling of autonomy, and of purpose. I am paid well enough that I am more motivated by doing cool things than I am my paycheck, and my company gives me a purposeful workday.
