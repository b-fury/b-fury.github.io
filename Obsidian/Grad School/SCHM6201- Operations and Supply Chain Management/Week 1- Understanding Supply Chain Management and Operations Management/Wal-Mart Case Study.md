Analyze the case study **Half a Century of Supply Chain Management at Walmart (Ivey, \#W12894)** and submit a four-page write-up on the following:

1. What attributes demonstrate the effectiveness of Walmart’s supply chain?
2. How does Walmart’s performance compare to its competitors? Is it performing better or worse? How?
3. Is Walmart's supply chain strategy and performance sustainable? What recommendation would you give to James Neuhausen?

  

1. The Walmart supply chain is truly remarkable. When this case study was written, there was essentially no greater logistical power than the US Military. From their outset, Sam Walton seemed to possess a keen instinct for what is efficient. All early stores were within a couple hours drive of the original distribution center, which allowed for rapid restocking of stores. Fast forward many years and this hub and spoke model applies to many successful companies. To centralize the storage of product and to keep track of each districts needs is the basis of a successful supply chain. ==Also central to the Every Day Low Price model is quick turnover of product and rapid targeted restocking, and when combined with the hub and spoke model, this allowed for a freedom that other vendors simple couldn’t compete with.== Walmart has since also expanded on the hub and spoke vision, with separate distribution centers focused on restocking rapidly depleting items, such as paper goods.
    
    As time went on, Walton also became interested in computing, and with that, the hub and spoke distribution model also because a “server and spreadsheet” model. Walton was one of the first to implement individual barcodes, which allowed for daily updates on what was and what wasn’t selling. When combined with the already existing hub and spoke model, Walmart now had individual store data, so the supply chain could further be streamlined. Walmart has since led the way on RFID implementation, allowing for goods to be monitored in an entirely automated and frictionless, providing near real-time data as items enter and leave the store.
    
    In the end, however every distribution channel needs trucks. This led to another impressive decision. Walton decided that they should own their own truck-based distribution channel. Most impressively is that these drivers are not unionized. Despite the power of the Teamsters union, Walmart has continued to treat their drivers well enough that that never unionized. In 2021, that is perhaps less impressive, but in the 70’s the teamsters were one of, if not, the most powerful union in the States. This lack of union has allowed for Walmart to rapidly implement changes and improvements without any friction.
    
      
    
    1. From a supply chain/ logistics perspective, Walmart has way more efficiency than their competitors. The likes of Sears and Kmart, could never compete, and were always left behind in terms of efficiency. For instance, Kmart has a distribution cost of 3.5% of total sales, which is more than double that of Walmart (1.7%). The only true competitor Walmart is Amazon, when it comes to order fulfillment. Amazon does not have a significant brick and mortar presence, but their information infrastructure is second to none. Walmart also started to hold significant power over its vendors, and once it got large enough has been able to force its vendors to produce Walmart-specific variants which they could further optimize for their supply chain.

    1. This strategy by Walmart is not very sustainable, at some point you can no longer be more efficient. As time goes on, and it struggles to break into smaller footprint city markets, most of its value disappears. With the rise in online shopping Walmart needs to restructure its hub and spoke model to have distribution centers that then deliver directly to homes. This tends to hold less ability to extract value, as last mile deliveries are costly from a fuel perspective, and requires a far more "personal" supply chain. As modern businesses have popped up focussing on last-mile logistics, companies such as Target have partnered with them for same-day deliveries [1]. While Walmart still retains in distribution chain benefits intact, it lost a large amount of value as same-day delivery has taken off, making brick and mortar stores less relevant.
